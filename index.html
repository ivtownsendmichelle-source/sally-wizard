Introduction

What is Sally?

Sally is a voice profile. It's a collection of real writing samples from your life, organized by who you're talking to and how formal you're being.

Once your Sally is built, it teaches an AI writing assistant to sound like you. Not a generic version of professional. Not a chatbot. You.

The way you text your best friend and the way you email your biggest client are both you. Sally captures both ends of that range and everything in between. You define what each level means. You describe how each format works. The whole thing is built around how you actually write.

This wizard walks you through it in five steps. There are no wrong answers. The more honest your samples, the better it works.

Step 3 of 5

Describe how each format works for you.

For each type of writing you selected, describe how you like to structure it. How does it open? What goes in the middle? How does it close? How long is it usually?

Nothing here is required. Skip anything that doesn't feel relevant.

Example — Newsletter: "I open with something personal that happened that week. Then the main topic. I close with a question or a call to action. Usually 300 to 500 words."

Example — Facebook post: "Short. One idea. No hashtags. Sometimes a question at the end."

Example — Client email: "I lead with their name, acknowledge what they reached out about, then get to the point. I always close with a next step."
Step 4 of 5

Define your levels.

Think of 1 as the most casual writing you do and 10 as the most formal. Fill in the ones that feel real to you. Leave the rest blank.

Level 1 — Texting my kids or my partner. No filter.
Level 4 — Friendly email to someone I know professionally.
Level 7 — A proposal or a pitch. Buttoned up.
Level 10 — Legal or official documentation.
